## Summary

Spatial software is undergoing the same transition as the rest of the software world: agents are entering the picture. Users increasingly expect to describe the outcome they want, rather than click through every tool and parameter.

For software vendors, this is harder to respond to than it sounds.

What agents actually mean for your product isn't obvious yet.

Generic AI playbooks and bolt-on chatbots don't cover geodata, GIS, or point clouds.

And a demo is the easy part - an agent your customers can rely on is what most teams have never shipped.

I help spatial software vendors nail this transition - as a contractor, hands-on, embedded with your team, from first experiment to production.

With a background in geoinformatics and geomatics for mining, I work with teams whose users manage farmland, mines, infrastructure, or 3D scans.
