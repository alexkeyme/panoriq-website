02 — What it does

Spatial work,
without the
scripting overhead.

Working with point clouds today means stitching together CloudCompare, PDAL, Python, and a stack of half-remembered shell commands.

Panoriq replaces that loop with a single agent you describe your intent to. It plans the operations, runs them, shows you the result, and lets you iterate until the data looks the way it should.

01 / Edit

Modify in language.

"Remove the trees on the north side." "Downsample to 1 cm in this region." Describe the change, get the result.

02 / Segment

Isolate what matters.

Identify, label, and extract regions — buildings, ground, vegetation — without writing a classifier from scratch.

03 / Query

Ask spatial questions.

"How tall is this structure?" "What's the volume of this cavity?" Get answers grounded in the underlying geometry.

03 — How it works

Three steps. No new
pipelines to learn.

step 01 / load

Point the agent at your data.

Local files or a remote scan. Nothing leaves your environment unless you explicitly send it somewhere — the agent runs against the data in place.

step 02 / instruct

Describe what you want.

Plain English. The agent breaks the request into operations, runs them, and shows a diff of what changed before you commit.

step 03 / iterate

Refine and export.

Undo, branch, refine. When the result is right, export to your format of choice or pipe it straight into the tools you already use.
