02 — What we do

Spatial work,
without
the scripting.

Working with point clouds today means stitching together CloudCompare, PDAL, Python, and a stack of half-remembered shell commands.

Panoriq replaces that loop with a single agent you describe your intent to. It plans the operations, runs them, shows you the result, and lets you iterate until the data looks the way it should.

See capabilities
03 — Capabilities

Four moves on a single horizon.

Edit, segment, query, and trust — the four operations that turn a raw point cloud into something you can act on.

Ⅰ — Edit

Modify in language

"Remove the trees on the north side." "Downsample to 1 cm in this region." Describe the change, get the result.

  • — Spatial filters, transforms, and crops
  • — Diff preview before anything is committed
Ⅱ — Segment

Isolate what matters

Identify, label, and extract regions — buildings, ground, vegetation — without writing a classifier from scratch.

  • — Class-aware extraction in one pass
  • — Refine labels by pointing, not coding
Ⅲ — Query

Ask spatial questions

"How tall is this structure?" "What's the volume of this cavity?" Get answers grounded in the underlying geometry, not approximated from a screenshot.

  • — Distances, volumes, surface areas
  • — Every answer linked to the points behind it
Ⅳ — Trust

Reproducible by default.

Every action the agent takes is recorded as a structured operation — re-runnable, auditable, diffable. Eval-driven, not vibes-driven.

  • — Full operation history, exportable as a script
  • — Local-first — your data stays where it is
04 — How it works

Three steps. No new
pipelines to learn.

Load your data, describe what you want, iterate until it's right. The agent handles the operations — you stay in the loop.

01

Load

Point the agent at your data — local files or a remote scan. Nothing leaves your environment unless you explicitly send it somewhere.

02

Instruct

Describe what you want in plain English. The agent breaks the request into operations, runs them, and shows a diff of what changed before you commit.

03

Iterate

Refine, branch, undo. When the result is right, export to your format of choice or pipe it straight into the tools you already use.
